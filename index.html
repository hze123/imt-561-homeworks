by Zhien He - source code

Context: This clock is designed for people who enjoy tea or people who are not familiar with different tea brewing times. It works as a visual tea timer that helps users choose a tea type and understand how long it should brew. The clock could be used on a kitchen tablet, phone screen, or small smart kitchen display while preparing tea.

Final Clock 1: Interactive Tea Brewing Clock

Design process:

  1. Tea cup as timer: I used the tea cup itself as the main timer. After the user clicks the cup, the liquid level rises to show brewing progress.
  2. Color-coded tea types: Different tea categories use different colors. Green tea is green, red / black tea is reddish brown, oolong is amber, and herbal tea is pink.
  3. Different brewing durations: Each tea has its own brewing time, so the clock works as a simple guide for people who are not familiar with tea brewing.
  4. Countdown on the cup: I placed the remaining time directly on the cup so the design stays easy to read while keeping the tea visual as the main focus.
  5. Motion and feedback: Steam, bubbles, and the progress ring make the clock feel more alive. These details show that time is actively passing.

Information visualization considerations: I used liquid height to encode timer progress, color to encode tea category, motion to encode seconds, and text hierarchy to make the most important information readable. The large countdown gives precise information, while the liquid level and progress ring provide a quick visual summary.

Self-reflection / future work: In the future, I would like to make the tea cup and steam look more realistic. I would also add a stronger ready notification and allow users to customize brewing time based on how strong they like their tea.

Context: This clock is designed for coffee lovers and people who are learning how to steam milk for different coffee drinks. It works as a visual milk-steaming timer for a coffee-making or kitchen setting. The clock helps users understand how different coffee styles and milk types affect steaming time, foam amount, and visual texture.

Final Clock 2: Coffee Milk Steaming Clock

Design process:

  1. Cup as timer: I used the coffee cup itself as the main timer. After the user clicks the cup, the milk level rises to show steaming progress.
  2. Coffee style as foam pattern: Different coffee drinks have different foam styles. Latte, cappuccino, and flat white each use different foam amounts and surface patterns to show how milk texture changes by drink type.
  3. Milk type as color change: Whole milk, oat milk, and almond milk slightly change the color of the drink. This makes the milk selection visible without adding too much clutter.
  4. Different steaming durations: Each coffee style has a different timer. This helps beginners understand that different drinks may need different steaming times and textures.
  5. Readable countdown: The remaining time appears directly on the cup, so the clock stays functional while still using the drink as the main visual object.
  6. Motion and feedback: Steam, bubbles, foam movement, and the progress ring make the clock feel active. These animated details show that time is passing and that the milk is being steamed.

Information visualization considerations: I used liquid height to encode timer progress, foam pattern to encode coffee style, color variation to encode milk type, and motion to encode seconds. The countdown provides precise time information, while the rising milk level and progress ring provide a quick visual summary. I also separated the status panel, cup, and buttons so the page is easier to read.

Self-reflection / future work: In the future, I would like to make the foam patterns more realistic and add a pouring interaction after the timer finishes. I would also add a clearer ready alert, such as a sound or stronger visual signal, so users know when the milk is ready to pour.

by Yongxi Chen — source code

Context of use: This sketch represents the rhythm of box breathing, a meditation technique used to calm the mind and reduce stress by maintaining a steady 4-4-4-4 breathing cycle: breathe in, hold, breathe out, hold. It is designed for mindfulness practice, stress management sessions, and focus exercises where users need visual and temporal guidance to regulate their breathing.

Ex 11

Design process:

  1. Square path visualization: The breathing cycle is represented by a square with four edges, where each edge corresponds to one breathing phase. A colored line progressively draws around the square, providing clear visual tracking of the 4-second intervals. The square shape reinforces the "box breathing" concept.
  2. Color-coded phases: Each of the four phases uses a distinct teal-to-gray gradient color (teal for inhale, light blue for first hold, mint for exhale, gray for second hold).
  3. Countdown timer with neon glow: Large text displays the current phase label. The neon glow effect on the lines creates a calming, modern aesthetic while maintaining high visibility against the starry background.

Self-reflection / future work: Add customizable breathing durations (e.g., 3-3-3-3 or 5-5-5-5 cycles) to accommodate different skill levels. Include audio cues or gentle sound at phase transitions to support eyes-closed practice.

by Teresa Wang — source code

Context: This clock is designed for people who love making pancakes. It works as a fun, visual timer to use while cooking, helping users track how long their pancakes have been frying through animation and color changes.

Ex 12

Design process: For my pancake clock, I designed the layout as a pancake cooking simulator. It shows a pan frying pancakes and then dropping them onto a plate, which makes the whole clock feel like a small kitchen scene. The animation was an intentional choice because it imitates a real cooking environment and makes the passage of time more visual and engaging.

The color change of the pancakes is another key design choice. They start light and gradually turn golden brown as time passes. This helps people understand how long the pancake has been cooking without needing to read numbers, giving a more natural sense of time. I also added a countdown counter below the plate to give users a clear, easy-to-read time reference, balancing fun visuals with functional feedback.

Since I wasn’t able to hand-draw detailed pancakes due to limited time, the visuals could have looked more polished if I had more time to refine them. I also chose warm, high-contrast colors (browns and yellows) for better visibility and accessibility.

Self-reflection / future work: In the future, I would like to add more realistic pancake textures and steam effects to make the cooking scene feel livelier. I also plan to add features like letting users flip the pancakes with a click to include simple interactivity.
